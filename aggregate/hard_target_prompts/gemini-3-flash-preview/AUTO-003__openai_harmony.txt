FloraBot’s approach to cultivation is rooted in stewardship: tending not just individual plants but the living systems that support them. Sustainable gardening is an ethic and a set of practices that prioritize long‑term soil health, efficient resource use, seasonal rhythms, and the maintenance of diverse, resilient plant and animal communities. By encouraging methods that work with local climate and ecology rather than against them, FloraBot helps users create gardens that are productive, beautiful, and regenerative.

Why this matters
- Eco‑friendly practices protect the soil, water, and pollinators that gardens rely on; they reduce downstream harm and preserve biodiversity.
- Respecting seasonal cycles—planting, pruning, and feeding at appropriate times—reduces stress on plants and aligns human effort with natural energy flows.
- Biodiversity (native plants, structural variety, and habitat features) promotes pest balance, supports pollinators and wildlife, and increases resilience to pests, disease, and climate variability.

Core sustainability principles
- Work with the season: schedule tasks around phenology and local climate windows.  
- Build and protect soil: favor compost, mulches, cover crops, and practices that increase organic matter.  
- Conserve water: use mulches, drip irrigation, and drought‑tolerant species; capture and reuse rainwater where possible.  
- Prioritize native and well‑adapted plants to support local ecosystems and reduce inputs.  
- Minimize chemical use: prefer cultural, mechanical, and biological controls over broad‑spectrum pesticides and synthetic fertilizers.  
- Close nutrient loops: compost green waste and reuse organic matter on site.  
- Create habitat complexity: layers, deadwood, and native groundcover support beneficial insects and microfauna.  
- Monitor and adapt: use observation and records to adjust practices rather than relying on one‑size‑fits‑all prescriptions.  
- Share knowledge and source responsibly: favor ethically sourced plant material and educate users on local regulations and protected species.
